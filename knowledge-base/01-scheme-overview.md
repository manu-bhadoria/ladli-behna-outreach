# 01 — Scheme Overview

## Name

- **English:** Chief Minister Ladli Behna Yojana
- **Hindi:** मुख्यमंत्री लाड़ली बहना योजना (Mukhyamantri Ladli Behna Yojana)
- **Common short form:** Ladli Behna / CMLBY
- **Not to be confused with:** *Ladli Laxmi Yojana* (a separate MP scheme for girl children, one-time ₹1.43 lakh at age 21) or *Ladki Bahin Yojana* (Maharashtra's separate scheme).

## One-line purpose

A Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) scheme that deposits a fixed monthly amount into the bank accounts of eligible women in Madhya Pradesh, aimed at economic self-reliance, nutrition, and health.

## Launch timeline

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Scheme announced | 28 January 2023 |
| Officially launched | 5 March 2023 |
| First installment credited | June 2023 |
| Amount hike to ₹1,250 | October 2023 (pre-election) |
| Amount hike to ₹1,500 | November 2024 (Diwali) |
| Government target of ₹3,000 / month | By 2028 (as stated by CM Mohan Yadav) |

## Implementing authority

- **Government:** Government of Madhya Pradesh
- **Launched under CM:** Shivraj Singh Chouhan (BJP)
- **Currently administered under CM:** Mohan Yadav (BJP)
- **Nodal department:** Department of Women and Child Development, Government of Madhya Pradesh

## Delivery model

- **Transfer type:** DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer) — monthly
- **Credited to:** Aadhaar-linked, DBT-enabled bank account of the beneficiary herself
- **Typical transfer day:** Around the 10th–12th of each month; the CM publicly releases the tranche in a staged event from a district HQ.

## Core value proposition (for outreach)

Unlike many central schemes that reimburse a cost or subsidise a specific asset, Ladli Behna puts **unconditional cash** directly in the woman's own bank account each month. That framing — *her own money, her own account, no receipts required* — is the scheme's primary appeal and the single most important fact to lead with.
