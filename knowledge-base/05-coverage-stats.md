# 05 — Coverage & Numbers

All figures below are spot values and drift month-to-month. Treat them as "as of April 2026" unless noted. For live figures, pull from the government's installment press releases.

## Headline numbers (April 2026)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Active beneficiaries | ~1.25 crore women |
| Peak beneficiary count ever recorded | ~1.31 crore |
| Monthly amount per beneficiary | ₹1,500 |
| Typical monthly tranche size | ₹1,800 – ₹1,900 crore |
| Cumulative transfers since launch (June 2023 – April 2026) | ₹52,304+ crore |
| State budget allocation FY 2026–27 | ₹23,882 crore |

## Enrollment trajectory

- **March 2023** (launch): 0
- **November 2023**: 1.31 crore women enrolled — the peak
- **April 2026**: ~1.25 crore active beneficiaries

The gap between peak enrollment and current active count reflects cumulative removals (age-out, deaths, ineligibility surfacing during verification) net of fresh additions from subsequent application windows.

## Cumulative removals (to date)

| Reason | Approx count |
|---|---|
| Crossed age 60 | ~3,04,000 |
| Deceased | ~15,748 |
| Other ineligibility (income/asset/govt job in family surfaced on verification) | ~70,000+ |
| **Total removed** | **~3,92,000** |

## Geographic distribution

Madhya Pradesh has 55 districts; enrollment is broadly proportional to female adult population, with higher uptake in:
- Rural blocks (where alternative cash income sources are limited)
- Tribal belts of western and eastern MP
- Districts with strong Samagra enrollment baselines

No district has been publicly excluded from the scheme. Beneficiary density in urban slums has historically lagged rural areas due to Samagra coverage gaps, not eligibility gaps.

## Fiscal weight

At ₹1,500/month × 1.25 crore beneficiaries × 12 months ≈ **₹22,500 crore/year** in direct transfers alone.

That is roughly **~7–8% of the total MP state budget** (state budget FY 2026–27 ≈ ₹3.65 lakh crore). Only a handful of state schemes anywhere in India come close to this share of a single state's budget.

The promised ₹3,000/month level (by 2028) would roughly **double** the annual outlay to ~₹45,000+ crore/year at current beneficiary counts — a number commonly raised in fiscal-sustainability commentary on the scheme.

## What these numbers mean for outreach

- "Reaches 1 in 2 adult women in the state" is a defensible statement — MP's adult female population is ~2.5 crore, and active beneficiaries are ~1.25 crore.
- "Largest unconditional cash-transfer scheme by any Indian state" was true for 2023–24 and remains broadly true, though Maharashtra's Ladki Bahin has since reached comparable raw beneficiary counts at a lower per-capita amount.
