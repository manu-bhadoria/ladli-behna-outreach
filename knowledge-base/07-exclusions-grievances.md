# 07 — Exclusions & Grievances

This file is the single most important one for field outreach. Most negative interactions with beneficiaries are about removal, non-credit, or eligibility disputes — not about what the scheme *is*.

## Why names get removed

Ordered by frequency:

1. **Age out (crossed 60)** — ~3.04 lakh cumulative removals. Automatic, triggered on DOB in Samagra.
2. **Death of beneficiary** — ~15,748 cumulative removals. Triggered via municipal death-registration feeds or family notification.
3. **Verification re-flag** — family income later found > ₹2.5L, IT filing detected, or asset disqualifier (4-wheeler / ≥5 acres / govt job in family) surfaces.
4. **Samagra mismatch** — Aadhaar–Samagra name/DOB mismatch or failed re-KYC.
5. **Duplicate entry** — same woman appearing under two Samagra IDs.
6. **Self-requested exit** — rare but happens, often when a family income change makes the woman a double-dipper.

## "I didn't get my money this month" — diagnostic order

Walk through in this sequence:

1. **Is she still on the active list?**
   Portal → Application Status → enter Samagra ID. If status shows *Removed* or *Rejected*, go to reason.
2. **Is her Samagra e-KYC current?** If e-KYC expired or was never completed, payment is held.
3. **Is her Aadhaar seeded AND DBT-mapped to the correct bank account?** Most "silent" non-credits are here. Multiple accounts under one Aadhaar means only the latest DBT-mapped one receives the credit.
4. **Is her bank account active?** Dormant accounts reject credit.
5. **Was this a tranche-wide delay?** Occasionally the state defers an installment by 5–10 days. Check local news before assuming an individual problem.
6. **Has SMS to registered mobile arrived?** If yes but no credit visible in passbook, it's a bank-side issue, not a scheme-side issue.

## "They said my mother is too old"

Age 60 is a hard ceiling in the current rules. Communication to family:
- The rule is statutory, not discretionary — the local official cannot override it.
- Other welfare instruments may be available: NSAP old-age pension, Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension, state-level widow pension. The woman may be eligible for these *in parallel* once she exits Ladli Behna.
- The state has occasionally floated extending the ceiling; do not promise this will happen.

## "They said our family has a car, but we don't"

This is typically a data-side error:
- Vehicle database is RTO-linked; a vehicle registered on a relative with the same address can cause false positives.
- The beneficiary can contest via the Gram Panchayat / Ward office with RTO proof. The scheme operator will initiate a re-verification request.

## "They said my husband is a government employee, but he's only contractual"

The disqualifier applies to **permanent/regular** government employees and pensioners. Contractual workers, outsourced staff, daily-wage staff, and MGNREGA workers are **not** disqualifying. If the portal has flagged this, it's a data error and is contestable with an employment-type certificate from the employing department.

## "Why is the amount still ₹1,500 and not ₹3,000?"

The ₹3,000 monthly amount is a **stated government target for 2028**, not a current entitlement. Outreach should never imply the ₹3,000 figure is active or imminent. If the audience raises it, confirm:
- Current amount: ₹1,500/month
- Target: ₹3,000/month
- Target date publicly stated by CM: 2028

## Grievance channels

1. **First point:** Gram Panchayat (rural) / Ward office (urban) — submit a written complaint with Samagra ID.
2. **District:** District Collector's grievance cell.
3. **State:** CM Helpline 181 (voice, 24x7, multi-lingual).
4. **Portal:** `cmladlibahna.mp.gov.in` → Grievance / Contact — for status escalation.

There is **no charge** for any application, e-KYC, grievance submission, or status check. Flag to beneficiaries if any intermediary asks for money.
