# 08 — Recent Updates

A running log of notable developments. Add dated entries at the top; do not delete old ones — the history helps when beneficiaries cite older numbers.

## April 2026

- **35th installment released** on ~12 April 2026 from **Ashta (Sehore district)** by CM Mohan Yadav.
- Tranche size: **₹1,836 crore** across **~1.25 crore** beneficiaries.
- Per-beneficiary amount: ₹1,500 (unchanged).

## March 2026

- **34th installment** transferred to accounts — amount per beneficiary ₹1,500, tranche ~₹1,800 crore.
- Media coverage coincided with renewed chatter on the ₹3,000 target. CM Mohan Yadav reiterated the 2028 timeline in at least two public addresses this month.

## State Budget 2026–27 (passed Feb 2026)

- **₹23,882 crore** allocated specifically to Ladli Behna Yojana continuation.
- Separate allocation for Ladli Laxmi Yojana (the distinct girl-child scheme) also preserved.

## Late 2025 / early 2026

- 10,963 women removed before the 23rd installment across multiple verification drives (2025 cumulative exclusions crossed ~3.92 lakh).
- Vijay Shah controversy resurfaced briefly; no formal rule change resulted.

## August 2025

- **27th installment** released **along with an additional ₹250 Rakhi Shagun bonus**, making the effective August credit ₹1,500 (₹1,250 + ₹250). This was before the permanent hike to ₹1,500.

## November 2024

- **Amount permanently raised from ₹1,250 to ₹1,500/month**, announced around Diwali by CM Mohan Yadav. This is the current operative amount.

## December 2023

- CM transition: **Mohan Yadav** sworn in, replacing Shivraj Singh Chouhan. Scheme preserved and expanded.

## November 2023

- BJP wins MP Assembly elections with 163 seats; Ladli Behna widely credited as the decisive factor with women voters.

## October 2023

- Amount raised from ₹1,000 to ₹1,250 in the pre-poll period.

## June 2023

- First installment credited — ₹1,000 to approximately 1.25 crore enrolled women.

## March 2023

- Scheme formally launched by CM Shivraj Singh Chouhan.

## January 2023

- Scheme announced (28 Jan 2023) in the lead-up to state budget.
