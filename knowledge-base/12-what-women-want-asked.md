# 12 — What women would LOVE to be asked when a CM voice bot calls

Research-grounded design input for the voice-call flow (`designs/cm-voice-call-flow.md` lives adjacent). Built from field quotes in `11-problems-deep.md`. This is the *content* layer; file 09 is tone/channel.

## Starting principle — three things the call must resolve

A cold call to a Ladli Behna beneficiary lands in a mind carrying three live tensions:

1. **"Is this a scam?"** — the first 10 seconds.
2. **"What does he want from me?"** — fear of attendance/loyalty tax, courtesy of the Vijay Shah incidents.
3. **"Finally, someone is asking?"** — if the call can switch on this signal fast, the rest flows.

Everything below serves lever #3. Get the call to feel like *being heard* rather than *being processed*, and the same 60 seconds produce a radically different emotional outcome.

## The question hierarchy (what she'd love to be asked)

Ranked by emotional impact per second, based on what women have actually told researchers.

### Tier 1 — the questions that change the room (USE ALL OF THESE)

| Question (Hindi) | Why it works |
|---|---|
| *"बहना, पैसा आपके अपने खाते में आ रहा है ना — आपके नाम पर?"* (*"Sister, is the money reaching your own account — in your name?"*) | Validates her as the account-holder, not a family intermediary. Opens the operational check (A2 failure mode) without being clinical. |
| *"आपके गांव में पानी की सुविधा ठीक है?"* (*"Is the water situation in your village okay?"*) | Anita Gond's exact complaint is the #1 unmet need in rural MP. Asking this signals the CM *already knows* it's the real issue behind the money. |
| *"आप क्या काम करती हैं — अपने हाथ से?"* (*"What work do you do — with your own hands?"*) | Answers Lakshmi Kaurav's demand for *"recognition of our livelihoods."* Re-frames her from recipient to worker. |
| *"कोई बात जो आप सरकार को बताना चाहें, जो हम नहीं पूछते?"* (*"Anything you'd like to tell the government that we don't ask?"*) | The *single most powerful question* on any call. Open-ended. Dignifying. Almost never asked by government. If the call does only one thing, do this. |

### Tier 2 — warm and specific (pick one or two)

- *"आपकी तबीयत कैसी है, बहना?"* (*"How is your health, sister?"*) — simple, human, non-invasive.
- *"क्या आयुष्मान कार्ड आपके पास है?"* — if she says no, genuine offer to help route it (since the CM apparatus can actually deliver this).
- *"बेटी अगर है तो वो पढ़ रही है?"* — only if the call has already established warmth. Asking about children too early reads as surveillance.
- *"इस महीने पैसे से सबसे काम की कौन सी चीज़ आई?"* (*"What's the most useful thing the money bought this month?"*) — invites her to speak about HER choice with the money. Affirms her agency (counters the "पैसा किसने लिया" failure — see anti-pattern below).
- *"आपके गांव से कलेक्टर / विधायक कभी आए हैं?"* — surfaces a gap the CM can actually close.

### Tier 3 — dignifying closers (end on one)

- *"आपकी बात मैं आज अपने कार्यालय में लेकर जा रहा हूं।"* (*"I'm taking your words to my office today."*) — if a specific issue was raised, this is the money line. Creates accountability the beneficiary can verify.
- *"अगली बार जब मैं उज्जैन / ग्वालियर / सागर आऊं तो आप मिलने आएंगी?"* — invitation, not summons. Flips the power dynamic entirely.
- *"बाबा महाकाल / नर्मदा मैया की कृपा आप पर बनी रहे।"* — cultural close. Works in MP, fails outside it.

## The hard do-not-ask list

These are the questions that unravel trust in under one sentence. **None of them should ever be in the flow.**

| ❌ Anti-pattern | Why it destroys the call |
|---|---|
| *"क्या आप कल सम्मान समारोह में आएंगी?"* / any attendance ask | Literally the Vijay Shah incident. Listener hears *"prove your loyalty to keep the money."* |
| *"क्या आपने इस बार वोट दिया था?"* | Turns a welfare call into a surveillance check. Illegal in letter for government channels during MCC; always corrosive. |
| *"आपके पति / बेटे का क्या काम है?"* | Re-centres the household on male earners — exactly the frame the scheme is supposed to invert. |
| *"आपके घर में कितने लोग हैं?"* | Demographic audit energy. Feels like someone's filling out a form about her while they talk. |
| *"क्या आपकी eKYC पूरी है?"* | She doesn't know what eKYC is. Makes her feel ignorant. If there's an operational issue, frame it as *"हम देखते हैं आपका खाता ठीक है ना"*. |
| *"पैसा किस पर खर्च करती हैं?"* | Implies surveillance of her spending. The money is unconditional by design — asking how she spent it re-inserts conditionality. **Re-frame:** instead ask what the money *enabled*, not what it was *spent on*. |
| Any question requiring literacy (portal, app, status, reference number) | Excludes the ~40% of beneficiaries who cannot read. Instantly humiliating. |
| Generic *"आप कैसी हैं?"* with no follow-through | She's heard this in every scam call. Needs to be paired with a specific follow-up to read as real. |

## The three framings that change everything

Research-grounded rules, each worth more than a dozen specific questions.

### 1 · Ask about her *work*, not just her *benefit*

Every woman on this list does work — farm labour, MGNREGA, tailoring, dairy, gathering firewood, running a home for 4–8 people. The Lakshmi Kaurav quote (*"recognition of our livelihoods"*) is asking for this specifically. A call that names her work elevates her; a call that only names her benefit flattens her.

### 2 · Ask what's missing, not just what's received

Shantibai: *"The scheme is good, but there are other needs...such as having access to basic facilities."* She doesn't want you to stop thanking her for the money; she wants you to *also* ask what else is broken. The CM voice asking *"और क्या कमी है गांव में?"* does in one sentence what a decade of grievance portals can't.

### 3 · Offer something she doesn't have to prove she deserves

Beneficiaries constantly perform eligibility — for Samagra, for eKYC, for banking, for the scheme itself. A call in the CM's voice can give her ONE thing that requires no proving: **attention for its own sake.** That's what the open-ended *"कोई बात जो आप सरकार को बताना चाहें?"* delivers. It costs nothing, it tests nothing, and it's the rarest thing government does for rural women.

## Minimal three-question call (if latency/budget forces austerity)

If only three questions fit:

1. **Validate:** *"पैसा आपके अपने खाते में ठीक से आ रहा है?"* (operational + dignity)
2. **Listen:** *"कोई बात जो आप सरकार से कहना चाहें?"* (open-ended, the money line)
3. **Close with a promise:** *"आपकी बात मैं नोट कर रहा हूं। धन्यवाद बहना।"* (accountability + close)

Three questions, ~25 seconds of her speech, one deliverable action from the state. This is the viable minimum.

## The measurable outcomes to target

Each call should be designed to maximise **three** signals, not one:

1. **Did she keep the line open to the end?** (trust proxy)
2. **Did she speak at least once for 3+ seconds?** (voice proxy)
3. **Did she raise a specific local issue?** (engagement proxy)

If all three fire, the call has worked — irrespective of what the CM voice actually said. The call's job is to *create space*, not to *deliver information*.
